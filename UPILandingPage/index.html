Indonesia University of Education

History

Universitas Pendidikan Indonesia was established on October 20, 1954 in Bandung, inaugurated by the Minister of Teaching Education, Mr. Muhammad Yamin. Originally named Perguruan Tinggi Pendidikan Guru (PTPG), it was established against the backdrop of the history of the nation's growth, which realized that efforts to educate and educate the nation were an important part of filling independence. Some of the reasons for the establishment of PTPG include: First, after Indonesia achieved its independence, the nation was hungry for education. Second, the need to prepare quality teachers and university standards to improve the quality of education that will pioneer the realization of a prosperous society.

The University's main building was started from the ruins of a villa called Villa Isola, which is a former building from before World War II. (During the struggle against the colonizers, this building was once used as the headquarters of freedom fighters). The ruins were rebuilt and later transformed into a magnificent building called Bumi Siliwangi with its original architectural style.
